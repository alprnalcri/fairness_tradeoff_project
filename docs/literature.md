# Literature Review

Algorithmic fairness has become an increasingly important topic in machine learning due to concerns regarding discrimination against protected groups.

Hardt et al. (2016) introduced **Equality of Opportunity**, proposing that individuals who qualify for a positive outcome should have equal chances of being correctly identified regardless of protected attributes.

Kamiran and Calders (2012) proposed the **Reweighing** technique, a preprocessing method that adjusts instance weights to reduce discrimination before model training.

Agarwal et al. (2018) developed **Exponentiated Gradient Reduction (ExpGrad)**, an in-processing approach that incorporates fairness constraints directly into model optimization.

Chouldechova (2017) demonstrated that different fairness definitions may be incompatible, highlighting the complexity of achieving fairness in practice.

Recent surveys such as Caton and Haas (2024) emphasize that fairness interventions should be evaluated alongside predictive performance due to the inherent fairness–accuracy trade-off.
